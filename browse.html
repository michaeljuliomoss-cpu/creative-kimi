What we keep on the shelves

Browse our sections

Eight thousand books, hand-picked, hand-priced, spread across a dozen rooms’ worth of shelving. Here is what you will find most of.

Literature
Novels and short stories, mostly mid-century, with a good run of Faber & Faber paperbacks and NYRB editions nobody else keeps in print.
Poetry
From Frank O’Hara to Osip Mandelstam — a small but considered shelf, strong on Eastern European and New York School poets.
Cookbooks
Vintage Gourmet issues, spiral-bound community cookbooks, and the odd out-of-print Olney — the kind you actually cook from.
Translation
Works in English translation, with a bias toward small presses, neglected languages, and the kind of book you will not find at Barnes & Noble.
NYRB Classics
The full backlist, give or take half a dozen. If NYRB printed it we have probably had a copy through the door at some point.
Used Paperbacks Under $5
The bin by the door. Everything under five dollars, all the time. Not curated, not alphabetized — just a pile of paperbacks in good faith.
